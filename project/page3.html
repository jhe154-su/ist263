Something You Need to Know about Barnes Center at Arch ar SU

Have you ever felt stressed in front of a desk in Bird at 3AM?
Have you ever thought that you have to deal with all the depression alone?
While Syracuse University persists in constructing a qualified academic environment for those who dreamed, mental health is also emphasized within the university culture.
In there, Barnes Center at The Arch always stands with and tries to provide all you need.

What can you do at Barnes Center?

You can almost complete everything at Barnes Center!
Wanna do some exercise to relax yourself from a morning 8 AM class?
Sure! Barnes Center involves a comprehensive collection of fitness equipment -- Tennis, basketball, swimming and even mountain climbing!
As for the health concern, Barnes Center has a team of professional medical counselors to 24/7 support students’ physical and psychological health.
More importantly, students are welcomed to explore various cultural event to fulfill their own need!
One major mission behind the center was to promote mental health and well-being for all the SU students. The category of the services includes:

How can students get involved in Barnes Center?

“Be Mindful, Be Active, Be Involved” is the slogan!
Meeting any schedules and diversity of the interest, Barnes Center provides multiple methods for students to engage in! The mission for this program is to encourage students to explore their own interest and balance their well-being between school work and mental health.
You could enjoy an afternoon of skiing at Drumlins Country Club. Or just chill out and enjoy a Saturday game night for Among Us!

Check Out What is Happening at Barnes Center

Barnes Center never limits students as a normal participant! You are always wanted as an essential member for Barnes community.

Be a Staff:
Eligible Syracuse University students could become a part-time employee for various position, including personal trainer, volunteer, front desk attendant, special event staff and so on!
More information could be checked through Handshake!

Be a Supporter for your Peer:
As part-time employee opportunity for Barnes Center, Peer Educators program allow students to assist in conduct health and wellness education for their peers. There are three tracks for the programs:
Be Wise
The education program concentrates on the proper use of alcohol.
PEEHRS
The education program concentrates on sexuality and relationships.
SAMHE
The education program concentrates on mental health empowerment.
Don’t hesitate and apply for what you feel passionate about by today!
